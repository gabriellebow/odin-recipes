Grandma's Rouladen

Description

Did you grow up with a German grandma (or Oma) that spent the summers trying to fatten you up? Do you miss the nostalgia that came along with dinner at Oma and Opa's? Got a new love interest you'd like to impress with your ability to follow a simple recipe?

You've come to the right place! This was a staple in my household - well my grandma's household - growing up. Your family -or potential love interest (winky face) will love the flavour that comes along with this authentic German recipe.

Ingredients

Rouladen

  • 8 slices top round beef approximately 4x6in in size and 1/4in thick
  • 1/3 cup yellow mustard
  • 8 slices of bacon
  • 8 pickles sliced lengthwise
  • 1 yellow onion diced
  • salt and pepper to taste

Gravy

  • 1 TBSP butter
  • 1 TBSP olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 yellow onion diced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 leek chopped
  • 1 carrot chopped
  • 1 celery stalk chopped
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 TBSP tomato paste
  • 1 TSP sugar
  • 1/2 TSP salt
  • 1/4 TSP pepper
  • 4 TBSP chilled butter
  • 2 TBSP corn starch + 2 TBSP water

Steps

  1. Prep ingredients
  2. Preheat oven to 325Â°F
  3. Lay the beef slices out on a work surface. Spread each beef slice with 2 teaspoons of mustard and sprinkle with a dash of salt and ground pepper.
  4. Place a strip of bacon on each beef slice parallel to the length of the beef. Place sliced pickles and chopped onions on each slice of beef.
  5. Roll up the beef slices, tucking in the sides as you go. Secure the beef rolls with toothpicks.
  6. Heat the butter and oil in a dutch oven or oven-safe pot or baking dish and brown the rouladen generously on all sides. Set the rouladen aside on a plate.
  7. *Do not remove the browned bits in the bottom of the pan (as this will make for a flavourful gravy).
  8. Add the onions to the pot and cook until softened and translucent - appoximately 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  9. Add the leek, carrot and celery to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes.
  10. Add the red wine and bring to a rapid boil for one minute. Reduce the heat to medium and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  11. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaf, sugar, salt and pepper.
  12. Nestle the beef rouladen in the pot.
  13. Transfer the oven-safe pot to the preheated oven and bake for about 90 minutes.
  14. Remove the rouladen from the pot and set aside.
  15. Pour the liquid and vegetables through a strainer and save the liquid.
  16. Return the strained liquid back to the pot and bring to a simmer.
  17. Mix 2TBSP cornstarch with 2TBSP water and add to the pot to thicken the gravy. Simmer wisking consistently until the gravy has thickened.
  18. Add chilled butter, whisking constantly, until the butter is melted. Add salt, pepper and mustard to taste.
  19. Carefully remove the toothpicks. Return rouladen to the gravy and heat through.
  20. Serve and enjoy.
